What is Diwali Mela?

The Biggest Ever Diwali Mela, brings to you a celebration of the festival at it's best and in the most vibrant and festive atmosphere. The Mela is organized in a large open area of the popular Fair Park grounds. It is a time of joy, enthusiasm, and cultural exchange. Key features of a Diwali Mela include: Food Stalls: Various food stalls are set up, offering a wide array of mouthwatering delicacies and traditional Indian sweets. People can savor an assortment of snacks, chaats, sweets, and other regional specialties. Shopping: Diwali Mela is a great opportunity for shopping, as numerous stalls sell a variety of items such as traditional clothes, jewelry, home decor, handicrafts, candles, and Diwali-specific items like diyas (clay lamps) and firecrackers.

Cultural Performances: The Mela features cultural performances, including folk dances, music, and plays that showcase the rich and diverse cultural heritage of India. Fun Activities: For children and families, there are fun activities like rides, games, and competitions that add to the entertainment factor. Fireworks: The celebration culminates with a spectacular display of fireworks, which adds a dazzling spectacle to the night sky. The Diwali Mela not only brings joy and happiness to the people attending but also plays a vital role in fostering a sense of community and unity. It allows people from various backgrounds to come together and celebrate the spirit of Diwali, promoting harmony and cultural exchange.
